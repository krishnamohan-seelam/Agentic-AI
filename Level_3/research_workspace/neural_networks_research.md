# History of Neural Networks

## Historical Origins and Key Milestones

- **1950s**: The concept of artificial neural networks (ANNs) was inspired by biological neural circuitry. The first implementation was by psychologist Frank Rosenblatt, who developed the **perceptron**.
- **1970s-1980s**: This period is often referred to as an "AI winter" due to a lack of research and funding for ANNs.
- **1986**: The backpropagation algorithm was popularized, allowing for more effective training of neural networks.
- **1990s**: The development of recurrent neural networks (RNNs) and convolutional neural networks (CNNs) renewed interest in ANNs.
- **2012**: The introduction of **AlexNet**, a deep neural network with many layers, significantly advanced image recognition tasks and is credited with sparking the current AI boom.
- **2017**: The introduction of the **transformer architecture**, which revolutionized natural language processing tasks.

## Recent Developments and Breakthroughs (2024-2025)

- **Liquid Networks**: These networks are revolutionizing healthcare by providing more adaptable and efficient models for medical diagnostics and treatment.
- **Language Representation**: Research from Google indicates that advancements in language representation within deep learning models could offer new frameworks for understanding brain neural processes.
- **Machine Unlearning**: A trend towards digital data forgetting, allowing models to forget specific data points, is gaining traction.
- **Interoperability**: There is a growing focus on the interoperability between different neural networks, enhancing their collaborative capabilities.
- **BRAIN Initiative**: The initiative continues to make strides in understanding brain functions and applying these insights to neural network development.

## Current State

Artificial neural networks are now integral to many modern AI systems, requiring large datasets and substantial computing power. However, their internal representations remain difficult to interpret.
